What is the difference between Bankruptcy and Insolvency?

Bankruptcy is a legal process that individuals or entities go through when they find themselves unable to repay their debts. It provides a fresh start by either eliminating certain debts or devising a manageable repayment plan. Insolvency, on the other hand, is a broader financial term that signifies a situation where your liabilities exceed your assets or when you're struggling to meet financial obligations. Bankruptcy is often a consequence of insolvency.
