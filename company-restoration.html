Process to Apply for Company Restoration

For Administrative Restoration:

  • Ensure that the company has been dissolved for less than six years from the date of the application.
  • Address all outstanding filing requirements, including Annual Returns and company accounts.
  • Pay any applicable fees and late filing penalties.
  • Submit an application for company restoration to Companies House.
  • Wait for the registrar of Companies to make a decision regarding restoration.

For Restoration by Court Order:

  • Assess whether administrative restoration is not feasible or if the benefits of restoration outweigh the costs.

  • Upon successful restoration, the company will be reinstated.

Types of Company Restoration

Administrative Restoration:

This type of restoration is available for companies that have been dissolved for less than six years from the date of the application. It is carried out under the provisions of the Companies Act 2006. Companies House, the registrar of Companies, has the discretion to decide whether administrative restoration is appropriate for the company. Typically, the company can be restored if all outstanding filing requirements are satisfied, along with the payment of fees and any late filing penalties. The company's name and registration number remain unchanged, provided that a similar name hasn't been registered in the meantime.

Restoration by Court Order

This option is more expensive and involves legal proceedings. Restoration by court order is usually considered when administrative restoration is not viable or when the benefits of restoration outweigh the associated costs. The process involves solicitors, court fees, and registrar's fees, often totaling a substantial amount. LegalRaahi can provide a quotation for services related to restoring a company by court order. Our legal team is well-versed in the procedures and requirements of this process.
