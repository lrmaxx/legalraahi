What is the Armed Forces Tribunal?

The Armed Forces Tribunal is an essential judicial body in India, established under the Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007. It serves as a beacon of justice for individuals subject to the Army Act, 1950, the Navy Act, 1957, and the Air Force Act, 1950. The tribunal plays a pivotal role in adjudicating disputes and complaints related to commission, appointments, enrolment, and conditions of service within the armed forces.

Armed Forces Tribunal (Amendment) Bill, 2012

In 2012, the Armed Forces Tribunal (Amendment) Bill was introduced in the Rajya Sabha. This bill proposed several amendments to the Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007, including:

  • Extending the tenure of the Chairperson and members of the Tribunal from four to five years, with no possibility of reappointment.
  • Raising the age limit of the Chairperson from 65 to 67 years and increasing the age limit of judicial members from 65 to 67 years.
  • Empowering the Tribunal with powers equivalent to those of a High Court regarding contempt of itself.
  • Application of the Contempt of Court Act, 1971, with certain modifications. References to a High Court would include the Tribunal, and mentions of the Advocate General would be construed as referring to the Attorney-General of India, Solicitor-General, or Additional Solicitor-General.

Here are some important facts about the Armed Forces Tribunal:

Regional Benches

To cater to the diverse geographical areas and jurisdictions, the AFT has regional benches across the country. These benches are situated in Chandigarh, Lucknow, Kolkata, Guwahati, Chennai, Kochi, Mumbai, Jaipur, Jabalpur, and Jammu, serving specific regions of India.

No Jurisdiction Over Paramilitary Forces

It's essential to note that the AFT's jurisdiction does not extend to paramilitary forces; it primarily focuses on matters concerning the armed forces.

Composition

The AFT comprises judicial members who are retired high court judges and administrative members who are either retired armed forces personnel or Judge Advocate Generals.

Dress Code

Members of the AFT adhere to a specific dress code, which includes a white shirt, collar band, and a black coat or jacket.

Statutory Body

The AFT is a statutory body, ensuring its authority and legality in addressing armed forces-related matters.

Principal Bench

The principal bench of the Armed Forces Tribunal is located in the heart of India, New Delhi.

Jurisdiction

The tribunal follows a procedure akin to that of the High Court of India, and it operates under the Armed Forces Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2008.
