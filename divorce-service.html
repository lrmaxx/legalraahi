Overview of Divorce and Separation Services

At LegalRaahi, we understand that the process of divorce or separation can be complex and emotionally challenging. Our comprehensive services guide you through each step of the legal procedures, ensuring that your rights and interests are protected.

Divorce Process with LegalRaahi

Step 1: File the Divorce Petition

The divorce process starts with a divorce petition. LegalRaahi assists you in filing a comprehensive petition, including details about residency requirements and legal reasons for divorce.

Step 2: Request Temporary Court Orders

LegalRaahi helps you request temporary orders for matters like child custody, support, and spousal support to ensure your immediate needs are addressed.

Step 3: File Proof of Service

We guide you through serving the divorce papers to your spouse and filing the necessary proof of service, a crucial step in the process.

Step 4: Negotiate a Settlement

LegalRaahi supports you in negotiating a fair settlement with your spouse, ensuring that issues related to child custody, support, and property division are resolved amicably.

Step 5: Go to Trial, If Necessary

In cases where negotiations fail, LegalRaahi prepares you for divorce trials, ensuring you are well-equipped to present your case.

Step 6: Finalize the Judgement

LegalRaahi assists you in the finalization of the judgement of divorce, ensuring that all aspects, including custody, support, and property division, are legally documented.

Other Complimentary Divorce Services

Separation Services

In addition to divorce services, LegalRaahi offers guidance for couples considering separation. We help you understand the differences between trial separation and legal separation, assisting you in making informed decisions based on your state's regulations.

Child Custody Assistance

When child custody is a concern, LegalRaahi provides guidance on the various types of child custody, including joint custody, sole custody, and custody with a third party. We ensure that the best interests of the child are at the forefront of any custody decisions.
