Overview of Cybercrime

Cybercrime encompasses criminal activities that revolve around computers, computer networks, or networked devices. While the primary motivation behind most cybercrimes is financial gain, there are instances where cybercriminals have other motives, such as political or personal reasons. These cybercrimes can be committed by individuals or organized groups, ranging from highly skilled hackers to novice perpetrators.

Cyber Stalking and Cyber Bullying

Cyber stalking involves unwarranted online harassment or continuous monitoring of an individual, while cyberbullying pertains to the use of digital platforms for intimidation, threats, or demeaning behaviour towards others. Other prevalent cybercrimes encompass email fraud, social media-related offences, crimes associated with mobile apps, business email compromise, data theft, net banking and ATM frauds, fraudulent phone calls, insurance frauds, lottery scams, illicit activities involving cryptocurrencies like bitcoins, and various forms of cheating.

Types of Cybercrime

Some of the common Cybercrimes are:

Phishing:

Phishing is a deceptive technique where cybercriminals employ misleading emails or messages to dupe individuals into divulging sensitive information or performing unauthorized actions.

Ransomware:

Ransomware is a malicious software that encrypts a victim's data and demands a ransom for decryption. This often results in significant data loss and financial harm.

Identity Theft:

Identity theft entails the unauthorized acquisition and usage of someone else's personal information for fraudulent activities.

Online Money Scams:

Online money scams are deceptive schemes on the internet, wherein fraudsters manipulate individuals into providing personal information or sending money based on false promises of financial gain.

Protecting Against Cybercrime:

To safeguard yourself against cybercrime, consider the following precautions:

Keep Software and OS Updated:

Regularly update your software and operating system to stay protected with the latest security patches.

Use Antivirus Software

Employ antivirus or comprehensive internet security solutions to scan, detect, and remove threats. Keep the antivirus software updated for optimum protection.

Use Strong Passwords:

Create and manage strong, unguessable passwords. Consider using a reputable password manager for added security.

Beware of Email Attachments:

Refrain from opening email attachments from unknown senders, as they can be potential carriers of malware.

Avoid Clicking on Suspicious Links:

Be cautious about clicking on links in spam emails or from untrusted sources to prevent falling victim to cybercrimes.

Guard Personal Information:

Never disclose personal data over insecure phone lines or email communication. Validate the person's identification before talking to them.

Be Mindful of Website URLs:

Scrutinize the legitimacy of website URLs before clicking. Avoid unfamiliar or spam-like links. Enable online transaction security features if available in your internet security product.

Monitor Bank Statements:

Regularly check your bank statements to detect any unfamiliar transactions, allowing you to address potential cybercrimes promptly.

How to File a Cyber Crime Complaint?

If you are a victim of cybercrime and wish to lodge a complaint, follow these steps:

  • Visit the [https://cybercrime.gov.in/](https://cybercrime.gov.in/) website.
  • Choose the relevant option: 'Report Women/Child-Related Crime' or 'Report Cyber Crime.'
  • Click 'File a Complaint' and then 'I Accept' after reviewing the terms and conditions and privacy policy.
  • Create a user ID, register your mobile number, and provide your name and state.
  • Select your area and register your complaint, providing all necessary details about the offense.
  • You will receive a unique reference number upon registering the complaint, which you can use to track the status of your case.
