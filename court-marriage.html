Overview of Court Marriage in India

Court marriage in India is a legal and straightforward method of solemnizing a marriage. It is conducted in a court of law, without religious or traditional ceremonies. This process offers couples the freedom to marry irrespective of their caste, religion, or creed. Court marriages adhere to the Special Marriage Act, which ensures the entire procedure is transparent and straightforward. Couples opting for court marriage must meet specific eligibility criteria, provide essential documents, and adhere to the laws governing their religion or beliefs. Overall, court marriage in India provides a modern and legally recognized way for couples to unite in matrimony.

What is the Complete Process for Court Marriage?

Step 1: Consult a Matrimonial Lawyer

To simplify the court marriage process, consult a matrimonial lawyer. The lawyer will assist you in completing paperwork, organizing documents, and guide you through the procedure.

Step 2: Notify the Marriage Registrar

You must send a marriage application to the marriage officer of the district where at least one of the partners has lived for more than 30 days. The application should be submitted 30 days before the court marriage and must be signed by both partners.

Step 3: Display the notice of your forthcoming marriage.

The marriage officer will post a notice of your intended marriage for 30 days. This notice is displayed at the marriage office and recorded in the "Marriage Notice Book."

Step 5: Objection to the Marriage

If anyone has objections to your marriage, they can raise them directly with the marriage officer under Section 7 of the Special Marriage Act, 1954. The marriage officer has the authority to investigate objections and halt the marriage process if necessary.

Step 6: Completion of Court Marriage

Court marriages are devoid of traditional rituals. Both partners must sign a declaration form of marriage in the presence of the marriage registrar and three witnesses to complete the marriage.

Eligibility Criteria for Court Marriage

Before you proceed with a court marriage in Delhi, it's essential to meet the following eligibility criteria:

Age Criteria

For a Male, the minimum age is 21 years whereas for a female, it is 18 years.

Mutual Consent

Both partners should willingly agree to marry each other without any pressure or force.

Non-Prohibited Relationships

Ensure that your relationship doesn't fall under any prohibited categories defined by different religions and Indian laws.

No Active Relationships

If either partner was previously married, make sure to obtain a divorce decree from the court or provide a death certificate in case of a deceased spouse.

Important Documents Required for Court Marriage:

For a court marriage in Delhi, both partners and witnesses will need to provide specific documents:

  • Documents for Male and Female Partners
  • Photos: Both male and female partners should have six passport-sized photos.
  • Identity Proof/ID Proof: You can use any of the following: PAN Card, Driving License, Passport (any one of them).
  • Address Proof: Acceptable documents include Aadhar Card, Rent Agreement, Voter ID Card, Gas Bill, or Electricity Bill (any one of them).
  • Birth Proof: A Class 10 Marksheet or Birth Certificate (Janm Praman Patra).
  • Divorce Decree:In case of a previous marriage, you'll need to submit the divorce decree for marriage.
  • Death Certificate: This is applicable when one partner was in a previous marriage, and the other spouse has passed away. The living partner must provide the death certificate of the deceased spouse.

Documents for Witnesses

  • Photos: Two passport-sized photos of each witness are required.
  • Identity Proof (ID Proof): Witnesses can provide their PAN Card, Driving License, or Passport (any one of them).
  • Address Proof:Similar to partners, witnesses can use Aadhar Card, Rent Agreement, Voter ID Card, Gas Bill, or Electricity Bill (any one of them).
